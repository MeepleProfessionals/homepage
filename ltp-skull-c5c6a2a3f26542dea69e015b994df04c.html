Skull is a bluffing and bidding game played with a set of coasters or cards. The goal is to correctly guess the number of cards you can flip over without revealing a "skull" card. Here's a concise explanation of how to play:
  1. Setup: Each player receives four coasters or cards—three with flowers and one with a skull symbol. These are placed face-down in front of them.
  1. Bidding: Players take turns clockwise, starting with the first player. On their turn, a player can either place a bid or make a challenge. Bidding goes around until all players but one have passed.
  1. Bidding Process: When bidding, a player states the number of cards they think they can flip over without revealing a skull. The bid must be higher than the previous bid or challenge. Players can also bid "zero" if they want to challenge without flipping any cards.
  1. Challenge: When challenged, the player must now flip over the number of cards they bid. If they reveal a skull, they lose one of their coasters/cards. If no skull is revealed, they keep their coasters/cards and flip over their mat.
  1. Winning: The game continues with players taking turns bidding or challenging. Players win by either winning two challenges or by being the last player remaining with coasters.
Remember, the game involves bluffing and reading your opponents. You can bid high to make others challenge or bid low to deceive them. Pay attention to the bidding patterns and player behavior to improve your chances of winning.
Have fun playing Skull!
